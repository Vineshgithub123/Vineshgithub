THRISSUR

Also known as the cultural capital of Kerala, Thrissur is a city, globally renowned for the popular and magnificent Pooram Festival. From ancient times, Thrissur with its cultural heritage and archaeological wealth has played a significant role in the political history of Kerala as well as South India.Thrissur as a cultural hub is home to the the prestigious Sahitya and Sangeetha Nataka Academies of Kerala.

Athirapally Waterfalls

Situated at a height of 80 feet near the Sholayar Ranges, the majestic Athirapally Falls are known as the Niagara Falls of India. The river flows all the way through the Vazhachal Forest toward the Arabian Sea. The visual of the mighty Sholayar Peaks as its backdrop is a commanding site. It is also a great place for Bird lovers as it is the only place where four endangered Hornbill species are seen

Vadakkumnathan Temple

One of the oldest temples in the state, the Vadakkumnathan temple is a classical example of the Kerala style of architecture and has many decorative murals and pieces of art. The land around the temple was once a grove of teak trees and hence is called Thekkinkad or teak forest. This is the venue of the world famous Pooram festival celebrated annually in April-May. The fireworks at the Pooram are a spectacular sight.
